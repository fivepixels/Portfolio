About

Based in Santa Monica, I’m an Interaction Design graduate from SMC who builds at the intersection of the physical and the virtual. My career is defined by two extremes: the structural discipline of aerospace metal fabrication and the cutting-edge world of stadium-scale Augmented Reality.

Over the last three years at Snap, I’ve designed and developed AR experiences for the world’s biggest stages. From the 2024 Summer Olympics and the Super Bowl to the WNBA and Pro Bowl, I’ve created games and immersive integrations that live on the "Big Screen." My work has brought top-tier partnerships to life for brands like Disney and the Los Angeles Rams, turning massive public spaces into interactive playgrounds.

Coming from a technical background in fabrication, I approach every project with a "builder’s mindset." I don’t just design for aesthetics; I design for process, scale, and the core relationship between humans and technology. Whether I’m prototyping a new UX flow or launching a global AR event, my goal is to create efficient, user-friendly systems that make the complex feel effortless.
